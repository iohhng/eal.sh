---
title: Right Module
---

Let $R$ be a ring. A *right $R$-module* is an abelian group $M$ equipped with a map $M \times R \to M$, denoted $(m, r) \mapsto m \cdot r$, which satisfies:

:::enumerate{style="(i)"}
1. $(m + n) \cdot r = m \cdot r + n \cdot r$:
2. $(m \cdot r) \cdot s = m \cdot (rs)$;
3. $m \cdot (r + s) = m \cdot r + m \cdot s$; and
4. $m \cdot 1 = m$
:::

for all $m, n \in M$ and $r, s \in R$.
